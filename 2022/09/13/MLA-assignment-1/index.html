1. Implement the linear regression algorithm as described in the lecture

I used pseudo-inverse (as described below) to calculate the parameters \[ \boldsymbol{y}=\left(y_1, \ldots, y_n\right)^{\top} \]

\[ \tilde{\boldsymbol{X}}=\left(\begin{array}{cccc} x_{11} & \ldots & x_{1 d} & 1 \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ x_{n 1} & \ldots & x_{n d} & 1 \end{array}\right) \]

\[ \left(w_1, \ldots, w_d, b\right)^{\top}=\left(\tilde{\boldsymbol{X}}^{\top} \tilde{\boldsymbol{X}}\right)^{-1} \tilde{\boldsymbol{X}}^{\top} \boldsymbol{y} \]

As in this task the dimension of x in the data set is 1,so the formulas up can be transformed into: \[ \boldsymbol{y}=\left(y_1, \ldots, y_n\right)^{\top} \]

If R^2 < 0 it means the hypothesis is smaller than training labels' mean, which means the model needs to be improved.
